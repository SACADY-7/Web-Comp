Why Sleep Matters

Sharper Mind

Sleep consolidates memories and clears brain fog. Students who sleep well score 10-15% higher on tests.

Example: Like saving files on a computer — sleep "saves" what you learned all day.

Stronger Body

  • Repairs muscles and tissues
  • Regulates blood sugar
  • Boosts immune system
  • Lowers heart disease risk by 30%

Happier Mood

Good sleep balances mood hormones. Well-rested people are 60% less likely to feel irritable.

Real talk: Ever snapped at someone after a bad night? That's sleep talking!

Healthy Weight

Sleep regulates hunger hormones. People who sleep 7+ hours eat 300 fewer calories/day on average.

Tip: Want to curb late-night snacking? Go to bed earlier!

Common Sleep Stealers

Insomnia

Difficulty falling or staying asleep. Affects 1 in 3 adults occasionally.

Signs: Taking >30 min to fall asleep, waking often, feeling tired despite 8 hours in bed.

Sleep Apnea

Breathing stops briefly during sleep. Can happen 30+ times per hour!

Red flags: Loud snoring, gasping, daytime sleepiness, morning headaches.

Warning: Untreated apnea increases heart attack risk by 5x.

Restless Legs

Uncomfortable urge to move legs, especially at night.

Feels like: Crawling, tingling, or aching in legs that only movement relieves.

Sleep Questions Answered

How much sleep do I really need?

Adults: 7-9 hours
Teens: 8-10 hours
Kids: 9-12 hours

Quality > quantity. 6 hours of deep sleep beats 9 hours of restless sleep.

Is napping good or bad?

Good: 20-30 minute power naps boost alertness.
Bad: Naps >1 hour or after 3 PM disrupt nighttime sleep.

Why do I wake up at 3 AM?

Common causes:

  • Stress/anxiety
  • Alcohol (causes rebound wakefulness)
  • Low blood sugar
  • Sleep apnea

Try: No food/alcohol 3 hours before bed, relaxation routine.

When should I see a doctor?

Seek help if:

  • You feel tired despite 7+ hours sleep
  • Snoring + daytime fatigue
  • Can't fall asleep most nights
  • Falling asleep during day activities
