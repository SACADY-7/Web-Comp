Stay Healthy Through Every Season

From spring allergies to winter flu — learn simple ways to protect yourself and your family when the weather changes.

Health Risks by Season

Spring

Main Risk: Allergies

Pollen explodes. Sneezing, itchy eyes, runny nose.

Summer

Main Risk: Heat-related illness

Dehydration, heat stroke, food poisoning.

Fall

Main Risk: Common cold

Back to school = germ central.

Winter

Main Risk: Flu & infections

Cold air + indoor crowds = virus paradise.

Common Seasonal Illnesses

The Common Cold

Over 200 viruses can cause a cold. Most common in fall and winter when we're indoors more.

Symptoms

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Mild fever (in kids)
  • Fatigue

How It Spreads

  • Droplets from coughs/sneezes
  • Touching contaminated surfaces
  • Hand-to-face contact

Home Remedies

  • Rest and drink fluids
  • Salt water gargle
  • Honey + warm water (not for babies under 1)
  • Steam inhalation

When to See a Doctor

  • Symptoms last >10 days
  • High fever (>102°F)
  • Severe ear or sinus pain
  • Wheezing or shortness of breath
Pro Tip: Wash hands for 20 seconds (sing "Happy Birthday" twice). It's your best defense!

Influenza (Flu)

More serious than a cold. Can lead to hospitalization, especially in kids, elderly, and those with chronic conditions.

Symptoms

  • Sudden high fever (100–104°F)
  • Body aches
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Dry cough
  • Headache

Transmission

  • Airborne droplets
  • Contaminated objects
  • Close contact

Prevention

  • Annual flu vaccine (best protection)
  • Avoid sick people
  • Cover coughs/sneezes

Medical Care

  • Antiviral meds (Tamiflu) if started early
  • Seek care if: difficulty breathing, chest pain, confusion, persistent high fever
Emergency Signs: Blue lips, severe dehydration, seizures, or confusion — go to ER immediately.

Seasonal Allergies (Hay Fever)

Your immune system overreacts to pollen, mold, or dust. Not contagious!

Triggers

  • Tree pollen (spring)
  • Grass pollen (summer)
  • Ragweed (fall)
  • Mold spores

Symptoms

  • Sneezing fits
  • Itchy, watery eyes
  • Runny nose (clear mucus)
  • Post-nasal drip

Relief

  • Antihistamines (Claritin, Zyrtec)
  • Nasal saline rinse
  • Stay indoors on high pollen days
  • Shower after being outside

Long-term

  • Allergy testing
  • Immunotherapy shots/drops

Other Seasonal Infections

Strep throat, sinus infections, and ear infections often follow colds.

Strep Throat

  • Severe sore throat
  • Fever >101°F
  • White patches on tonsils
  • Needs antibiotics

Sinus Infection

  • Facial pain/pressure
  • Thick yellow/green mucus
  • Bad breath
  • Lasts >10 days

Ear Infection

  • Ear pain
  • Fluid drainage
  • Hearing loss
  • Common in kids

Prevention

  • Treat colds early
  • Avoid smoke
  • Use humidifier

Daily Prevention Checklist

Wash hands for 20 seconds with soap — after bathroom, before eating, after coughing.
Cover coughs/sneezes with tissue or elbow — not hands.
Avoid touching face — eyes, nose, mouth are virus highways.
Clean high-touch surfaces — doorknobs, phones, remotes.
Stay home when sick — protect others.
Get vaccinated — flu, COVID, pneumonia (if eligible).

Boost Your Natural Defenses

Sleep 7–9 Hours

Immune cells recharge at night. Lack of sleep = weaker defense.

Eat Colorful Foods

Vitamin C (oranges), Zinc (nuts), Probiotics (yogurt) fight viruses.

Move Daily

30 min walk boosts immune cells. Even light exercise helps.

Manage Stress

Chronic stress lowers immunity. Try deep breathing or meditation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I catch a cold from cold weather?

No! Colds come from viruses, not temperature. But cold air can dry nasal passages, making infection easier.

Should I take antibiotics for flu?

No. Antibiotics kill bacteria, not viruses. Flu is viral. Antivirals like Tamiflu may help if started early.

How long am I contagious?

Cold: 1–2 days before symptoms, up to 7 days after. Flu: 1 day before, 5–7 days after fever starts.

Are allergies worse in the morning?

Yes! Pollen counts peak between 5–10 AM. Keep windows closed during these hours.
