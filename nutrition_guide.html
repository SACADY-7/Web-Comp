Why Nutrition Matters

Energy & Growth

Food is fuel. Nutrients help kids grow and adults stay active.

Strong Immunity

Vitamin C, zinc, and protein fight infections.

Disease Prevention

70% of diabetes, heart disease preventable with diet (WHO).

Essential Nutrients & Food Groups

Carbohydrates

Energy source: Rice, roti, millets, potatoes.

Proteins

Build muscles: Dal, paneer, eggs, chicken, soy.

Fats

Healthy fats: Ghee, nuts, oil (in moderation).

Vitamins & Minerals

Fruits, greens, dairy, nuts.

Practical Tips & Meal Ideas

Breakfast

Snacks

Read Labels

Check sugar, salt, oil content.

Myth vs Fact

Myth: Carbs are always bad

Whole grains give energy. Avoid refined flour.

Fact: Choose millets, brown rice

ICMR recommends 50–60% carbs from whole sources.

Myth: Fats make you fat

Healthy fats are essential.

Fact: Ghee, nuts, oil in moderation

WHO: 30% calories from fats.

When You Should Seek Help

Children

6 meals/day. Include milk, eggs, fruits.

Pregnant Women

Extra 300 kcal, iron, folic acid.

Diabetes

Small frequent meals, low GI foods.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many meals a day?
3 main + 2–3 snacks. Don’t skip breakfast.
Are supplements needed?
Only if deficient. Get nutrients from food first.
Healthy on a budget?
Yes! Use seasonal veggies, dal, millets.
