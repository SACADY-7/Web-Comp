Water: Your Body’s Best Friend

60% of your body is water. Skip it, and everything slows down. Drink enough, and you’ll feel sharper, stronger, and happier.

What Happens When You Don’t Drink Enough

Brain Fog

Just 2% dehydration = 20% drop in focus. Like trying to work with a foggy windshield.

Constant Fatigue

Low water = thick blood = tired heart. You feel exhausted even after 8 hours sleep.

Kidney Stress

Dehydrated kidneys work 2x harder. Risk of stones up 30% with chronic low intake.

Weight Gain

Thirst = hunger in disguise. Many overeat because they’re actually thirsty.

Overheating

No water = no sweat = body temp rises. Heatstroke risk skyrockets.

Joint Pain

Cartilage needs water to stay cushy. Dry joints = creaky, painful movement.

Spot the Warning Signs

Dark Urine

Pale yellow = hydrated. Dark yellow/amber = drink now!

Dizziness

Standing up makes you woozy? Low blood volume from dehydration.

Dry Mouth & Skin

Skin loses bounce. Lips crack. Thirsty all the time.

Headaches

Brain shrinks slightly when dehydrated → painful pressure.

Water Myths Busted

Is 8 glasses a hard rule?

No! It’s a guideline. Your needs depend on:

  • Body size
  • Activity
  • Climate
  • Diet (soups, fruits count!)
Does coffee dehydrate you?

Myth! Coffee, tea, and milk count toward hydration. Just don’t replace all water with them.

Can I drink too much water?

Rarely. Over 1 gallon/hour can dilute sodium (hyponatremia). Spread intake throughout the day.

What about sparkling water?

Yes! Plain sparkling water hydrates just as well. Avoid sugary sodas.
